About Me

Procurement with Purpose

Thanks for stopping by — I'm glad you're here. Here's a bit of my story.

I'm a procurement professional who believes the best deals aren't just about price — they're about building the kind of supplier relationships that hold up when things get hard. That mindset has defined my career since I stepped off the campus of North Carolina A&T State University with a degree in Industrial and Systems Engineering.

Today at Inspire Brands, I lead indirect procurement for four major restaurant brands, managing one of the largest non-food spend portfolios in the QSR space. My work spans end-to-end category strategy, supplier negotiations, risk mitigation, and cross-functional partnership — all aimed at delivering measurable savings without compromising supply continuity.

My earlier career at JLL, Texas Instruments, and internships with Frito-Lay, GE Aviation, NextEra Energy, and Toyota Manufacturing gave me a deep appreciation for the complexity of global supply chains and the power of strategic sourcing done right.

How I Work

My Approach to Procurement

Three principles that guide every sourcing decision I make.

📊

Data First, Then Negotiate

Every negotiation starts with data — market benchmarks, BLS indexes, historical spend patterns, and supplier cost models. I don't guess at leverage; I build it with evidence.

🤝

Relationships Over Transactions

Suppliers aren't adversaries — they're strategic partners. I invest in transparent, long-term relationships because that's what delivers value when markets shift and supply chains strain.

🛡️

Protect the Business Proactively

I don't wait for disruptions to react. Whether it's a port strike, tariff escalation, or raw material shortage — I build contingencies, dual-source, and negotiate protection ahead of time.
